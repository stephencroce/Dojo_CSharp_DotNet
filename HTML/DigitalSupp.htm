Clinical Perspectives and Considerations With Retisert® (fluocinolone acetonide intravitreal implant) 0.59 mg in the Management of Chronic Non-infectious Uveitis of the Posterior Segment: Expertise Beyond the 3-year Follow-up Clinical Trial Data
Sponsored by: Bausch & Lomb
Presenters: Quan Dong Nguyen, MD, MSc, Panel Chair David G. Callanan, MD Debra A. Goldstein, MD, FRCSC Dean Eliott, MD Peter A. Netland, MD, PhD David G. Godfrey, MD Sunil K. Srivastava, MD

An expert panel of 7 uveitis, retina, and glaucoma specialists gathered in Chicago in August of 2009 to discuss the appropriate indications and applications of Retisert® (fluocinolone acetonide intravitreal implant) 0.59 mg. These specialists have extensive experience with the diagnosis and management of chronic non-infectious uveitis, the surgical placement and/or reimplantation of Retisert, and the medical and surgical management of ocular adverse events after implantation. In addition, two of the panel members were also investigators as part of the Fluocinolone Acetonide Study Group (Drs Callanan and Goldstein).
